DR.PETER C. ADAMSON

Dr. Peter C. Adamson is a pediatric oncologist and clinical researcher who has played a significant role in advancing the field of childhood cancer research. He received his medical degree from Cornell University Medical College and completed his pediatric residency at Boston Children's Hospital. Dr. Adamson's contributions to pediatric oncology are multi-faceted. One of his most notable roles was serving as Chair of the Children's Oncology Group (COG) from 2009 to 2014. COG is the world's largest organization devoted to childhood cancer research, conducting clinical trials to improve treatment and outcomes for children with cancer. During his tenure as Chair, Dr. Adamson focused on improving the design and execution of clinical trials to ensure that they yield reliable and relevant results. Under his leadership, COG prioritized the development of targeted therapies and personalized medicine approaches for childhood cancer. Dr. Adamson also emphasized the importance of international collaboration in pediatric oncology research. By fostering partnerships with researchers and institutions worldwide, COG was able to expand its reach and impact. In addition to his work with COG, Dr. Adamson has been involved in various other initiatives and committees aimed at advancing pediatric cancer research. His commitment to evidence-based medicine and patient-centered care has made a lasting impact on the field of pediatric oncology.

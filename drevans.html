DR.AUDREY E. EVANS

Dr. Audrey E. Evans was a pioneering pediatric oncologist whose work has had a profound impact on the treatment of childhood cancer. Born in 1925, she attended medical school at the Woman's Medical College of Pennsylvania (now part of Drexel University College of Medicine). In the 1950s, she began her career at the Children's Hospital of Philadelphia (CHOP), where she encountered children suffering from leukemia. At that time, childhood leukemia was considered incurable, with a very low survival rate. Dr. Evans was determined to change that. She teamed up with other researchers, including Dr. Werner Kalow and Dr. Joseph Burchenal, to investigate new treatment approaches for leukemia. Their collaboration led to the development of the first successful combination chemotherapy for childhood leukemia. Combination chemotherapy involves using multiple drugs with different mechanisms of action to attack cancer cells more effectively. By combining drugs like methotrexate, 6-mercaptopurine, and prednisone, they were able to induce remission in children with acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL) and significantly improve their chances of survival. In 1972, Dr. Evans co-founded the Children's Cancer Research Foundation, which later became part of the Children's Oncology Group (COG). COG is a collaborative research organization that brings together pediatric oncologists and researchers from around the world to conduct clinical trials and advance the field of pediatric oncology. Dr. Evans's dedication and pioneering work in childhood cancer research not only improved survival rates for children with leukemia but also laid the groundwork for modern pediatric oncology. She passed away in 2019, leaving behind a legacy of hope and progress in the fight against childhood cancer.
